Long are flights from Los Angeles to Australia?

The flight time from Los Angeles, California to Australia varies depending on the specific city in Australia you are flying to. However, on average, a non-stop flight from Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) to Sydney, Australia takes around 17 hours. A flight from Los Angeles to Melbourne, Australia takes around 16 hours and 30 minutes, and a flight from Los Angeles to Brisbane, Australia takes around 16 hours and 15 minutes. These flight times can also vary depending on factors such as the airline, aircraft type and weather conditions.
 

When are the Cheapest Flights from LAX to Australia?

The cheapest flights from LAX to Australia can vary depending on a number of factors such as the time of year, the airline and the specific route. Generally, the best time to find cheap flights from LAX to Australia is during the shoulder seasons, which are the periods between the peak tourist seasons. For example, September to November, and March to May are considered as shoulder seasons for travel to Australia, and during these times, flights tend to be cheaper.

Another way to find cheaper flights is to book well in advance, as airfares tend to increase closer to the departure date. Also, being flexible with your travel dates and route could also get you a better deal.

It is also recommended to check prices of multiple airlines, use fare comparison websites and sign up for fare alerts to stay informed about the cheapest flights.

How many Flights to Australia does Qantas have out of LAX daily?

Qantas operates flights between Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) and multiple cities in Australia, such as Sydney and Melbourne, but the frequency of flights can vary depending on the time of year and other factors.

As of my knowledge cut-off in 2021, Qantas has one daily non-stop flight from Los Angeles to Sydney and one weekly non-stop flight from Los Angeles to Melbourne. The airline also codeshares with American Airlines which operates more flights from LAX to Sydney and Melbourne. These flights can be subject to change due to the current situation of the COVID-19 pandemic and other factors, so it's best to check with Qantas for the most up-to-date information on flight schedules and availability.

From which terminal at LAX do American airlines flights to Australia leave?

American Airlines flights to Australia depart from Terminal 4 at Los Angeles International Airport (LAX). Terminal 4 is also known as the Tom Bradley International Terminal, which serves as the main terminal for American Airlines international flights, including flights to Australia. It's located on the west side of the airport, and is accessible via the LAX shuttle buses, parking structures, and the metro rail system.

It's always a good idea to check your flight itinerary, as well as the airport's website, to confirm the terminal and gate information for your flight, as it may change due to operational reasons.

American Airlines also operates codeshare flights with Qantas which are operated by Qantas, so you should check with Qantas for the departure terminal.
