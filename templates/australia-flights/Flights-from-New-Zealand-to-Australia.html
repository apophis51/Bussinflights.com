Flights between New Zealand and Australia are quite frequent and are operated by several airlines including Air New Zealand, Qantas, and Jetstar. These flights connect major cities in New Zealand such as Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch with major cities in Australia such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.

The flight duration between the two countries can vary depending on the specific route. For example, a flight from Auckland to Sydney takes around 3 hours and 20 minutes, while a flight from Christchurch to Melbourne takes around 3 hours and 50 minutes.

When it comes to the prices, they can vary depending on the time of booking, demand, and the airline. Budget airlines like Jetstar tend to have cheaper flights, but additional charges for baggage, seat selection and other services may apply. Other major airlines like Air New Zealand and Qantas also offer competitive prices and a variety of services.

It is important to compare prices and services from different airlines before making a decision. Travel websites such as Expedia, Kayak, or the website of the airline you wish to fly with can help you find the best deals.

In terms of international travel restrictions, please note that due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, some flights may be cancelled or temporarily suspended, and travel restrictions may be in place. It's important to check the latest information from the government and the airlines before planning your trip.
